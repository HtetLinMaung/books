##

Kubernetes offers different deployment models, including self-hosted, managed, and hybrid.

Self-hosted Kubernetes refers to deploying and managing Kubernetes clusters on your own infrastructure. This deployment model provides full control over the Kubernetes environment and allows for customization to meet specific business needs. However, it requires significant time and resources to manage and maintain the Kubernetes clusters.

Managed Kubernetes, also known as Kubernetes-as-a-Service, is a deployment model where a third-party provider manages the Kubernetes environment. Managed Kubernetes providers offer fully-managed, highly available Kubernetes clusters that can be scaled up or down based on the needs of the applications. This deployment model eliminates the need for managing the underlying infrastructure and provides an efficient and cost-effective solution for organizations.

Hybrid Kubernetes deployment model is a combination of self-hosted and managed Kubernetes. This model enables organizations to manage some Kubernetes clusters on their own infrastructure while relying on a third-party provider for others. This model offers the benefits of both deployment models while minimizing their respective drawbacks.
